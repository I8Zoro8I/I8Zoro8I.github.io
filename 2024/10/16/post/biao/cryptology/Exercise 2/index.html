2.1

Let \(f(x)=(x-a_1)(x-a_2)(x-a_3)\) be a monic cubic polynomial with roots \(a_1, a_2, a_3\), then the constant term of \(f\) is \(-a_1a_2a_3\).

Let \(E\) be an elliptic curve defined by \(y^2=x^3+Ax+B\). Let \(P_1=(x_1,y_1)\) and \(P_2=(x_2,y_2)\) be points on \(E\) with \(P_1,P_2\neq \infty\) and define \(P_1+P_2=P_3=(x_3,y_3)\). Define a polynomial \(F(x,y)=x^3+Ax+B-y^2=0\) based on the equation of \(E\). Let the line through \(P_1\) and \(P_2\) is \(y=l(x)=m(x-x_1)+y_1\), where \(m=(y_2-y_1)/(x_2-x_1)\). Then we have \[ F(x,y)=F\left(x,l(x)\right)=x^3+Ax+B-(mx+y_1-mx_1)^2=(x-x_1)(x-x_2)(x-x_3). \] Thus, \[x_3=\frac{(x_1y_2-x_2y_1)^2-B(x_1-x_2)^2}{x_1x_2(x_1-x_2)^2},\quad y_3=m(x_1-x_3)-y_1.\]

​

Direct calculations can prove that \(x_3=m^2-x_1-x_2\).

2.7

The point \((0,c)\) is the identity for the group law on \(C\), the addition law is \[ (u_1,v_1)+(u_2,v_2)=\left(\frac{u_1v_2+u_2v_1}{c(1+du_1u_2v_1v_2)},\frac{v_1v_2-u_1u_2}{c(1-du_1u_2v_1v_2)}\right). \] Since \((c,0)+(c,0)=(0,-c)\) and \((0,-c)+(0,-c)=(0,c)\), 4 is the smallest positive integer such that \(n(c,0)=\infty\), thus the point \((c,0)\) has order 4.

We are done.

2.9

\(E\) is an elliptic curve defined by \(y^2+a_1xy+a_3y=x^3+a_2x^2+a_4x+a_6\), the vertical line through \(P=(x_0,y_0)\) is \(x=x_0\). The line and curve intersect at \(P=(x_0,y_0)\) and \(-P=(x_0,y_1)\), substituting \(x=x_0\) into the equation of elliptic curve. We have \(y_0+y_1=-a_1x_0-a_3\), thus \(-P=(x_0,-a_1x_0-a_3-y_0)\).

2.10

It is obvious that \((x,y,z), (-x,-y,-z)\in \mathbf{R}^3\) map into \((x:y:z)\in \mathbf{P^2_R}\).

2.11

If the line \(a_1x+b_1y+c_1z=0\) is parallel with the line \(a_2x+b_2y+c_2z=0\), then they intersect at point \((b_1:-a_1:0)=(b_2:-a_2:0)\). Otherwise, they intersect at point \((b_2c_1-b_1c_2:a_1c_2-a_2c_1:a_2b_1-a_1b_2)\).

Let \(P_1, P_2\) be two distinct points on \(P(K)\) and \(u,v\) be representative vectors. Then \(u,v\) are linearly independent for otherwise \(u=\lambda v\) and \(P_1=P_2\).\ Let \(U\in K\) be the 2-dimensional vector space spanned by \(u\) and \(v\), then \(P(U)\in P(K)\) is a line containing \(P_1\) and \(P_2\).\ Suppose \(P(U^{\prime})\) is another such line containing \(P_1\) and \(P_2\), then \(u,v\in U^{\prime}\) and so the space spanned by \(u,v\) (namely \(U\)) is the subspace of \(U^{\prime}\). But \(U\) and \(U^{\prime}\) are 2-dimensional so \(U=U^{\prime}\) and the line is thus unique.

2.12

Since \((a,b,c)M=(a,b,c) \left( \begin{array}{c c} a_1 & b_1\\ a_2 & b_2\\ a_3 & b_3 \end{array} \right)=\left(aa_1+ba_2+ca_3,ab_1+bb_2+cb_3\right)=(0,0)\), then \(ax+by+cz=a(a_1u+b_1v)+b(a_2u+b_2v)+c(a_3u+b_3v)=(aa_1+ba_2+ca_3)u+(ab_1+bb_2+cb_3)v=0\).

2.13

Legendre equation is \(y^2=x(x-1)(x-\lambda)=y^2=x^3-(\lambda+1)x^2+\lambda x\), where \[ \begin{aligned} b_2&=a^2_1+4a_2=-4(\lambda+1),\\ b_4&=2a_4+a_1a_3=2\lambda,\\ b_6&=a^2_3+4a_6=0,\\ b_8&=a^2_1a_6+4a_2a_6-a_1a_3a_4+a_2a^2_3-a^2_4=-\lambda^2,\\ c_4&=b^2_2-24b_4=16(\lambda+1)^2-48\lambda=2^4(\lambda^2-\lambda+1),\\ \Delta&=-b^2_2b_8-8b^3_4-27b^2_6+9b_2b_4b_6=2^4\lambda^2(\lambda+1)^2-2^6\lambda^3=2^4\lambda^2(\lambda-1)^2. \end{aligned} \] Thus, the \(j\)-invariant is \[ j=\frac{c^3_4}{\Delta}=2^8\frac{(\lambda^2-\lambda+1)^3}{\lambda^2(\lambda-1)^2}. \]

2.14

Let \(u=1+t, v=mt\) for some \(m\), then \(u^2-v^2=(1+t)^2-(mt)^2=(1-m^2)t^2+2t=0\). Discarding the solution \(t=0\), we obtain \(t=2/(m^2-1)\), hence \[ u=1+t=\frac{m^2+1}{m^2-1},\quad v=mt=\frac{2m}{m^2-1}. \]

Let \(w=0\), then \(u^2-v^2=w^2=0\), hence \(u=\pm v\). Thus, the projective curve has two point at infinity, \((1:1:0)\) and \((1:-1:0)\).

Since the parameterization obtained in (a) can be written in projective coordinates as \((u:v:w)=(m^2+1:2m:m^2-1)\), then the values \(m=\pm 1\) correspond to points \((1:\pm 1:0)\), that is, correspond to the points at infinity. In the graph, we can find that \(u=\pm v\) is asymptote to hyperbola \(u^2-v^2=1\), they intersect at infinity.

2.15

Let \(L\) be the line through \((u_0,0,0)\) with slope \(m\): \[ u=u_0+t,\qquad v=mt. \] This yields \(a(u_0+t)^2+bm^2t^2=e\), which yields \((a+bm^2)t^2+2au_0t=0\). Discarding the solution \(t=0\), we obtain \(t=-2au_0/(a+bm^2)\). Substitute the expression just obtained for \(u\) to obtain \[ dw^2=f-(\frac{bu_0m^2-au_0}{a+bm^2})^2. \] This may be rewritten as \[ \begin{aligned} (a+bm^2)^2w^2&=\frac{(a+bm^2)^2}{d}f-\frac{c(au_0-bu_0m^2)^2}{d}\\ &=(\frac{b^2f}{d}-\frac{b^2u_0^2c}{d})m^4+\cdots \end{aligned} \] Let \(r=(a+bm^2)w\), then we obtain \(r^2=2ab(f+u_0^2c)/d m^2+k_2m+k_3\) for some \(k_2,k_3\), which has the form "square=degree2 polynomial in \(m\)".

The Jacobian matrix \(\left( \begin{array}{c c c} F_u & F_v & F_w\\ G_u & G_v & G_w \end{array} \right)_{(u_0,0,0)}= \left( \begin{array}{c c c} 2au_0 & 0 & 0\\ 2cu_0 & 0 & 0 \end{array} \right)\), hence the Jacobian matrix at \((u_0,0,0)\) has rank 1. Since the rank is less than 2, this means that the point is a singular point.

2.18

If \(a_2=a_4=0\), then the equation of elliptic curve is \(f=y^2-x^3-a_6=0\). We obtain \(\partial f/\partial y=2y, \partial f/\partial x=-3x^2\) and \(\partial f/\partial y, \partial f/\partial x\) vanish at \(P\in E[2]\), which is not allowed.

The change of variables \(x=x_1+(a_4/a_2)\) yields the equation \[y_1^2=\left(x_1+\frac{a_4}{a_2}\right)^3+a_2\left(x_1+\frac{a_4}{a_2}\right)^2+a_4\left(x_1+\frac{a_4}{a_2}\right)+a_6.\] The coefficient of \(x\) is \(3a^2_4/a^2_2+3a_4=0\), hence the equation has the form \(y^2_1=x^3_1+a_2^{\prime}x^2_1+a_6^{\prime}\).

The \(j\)-invariant of elliptic curve \(y^2=x^3+a_2x^2+a_6\) is \[ j=-\frac{a^3_2}{a_6}. \] If \(-a^3_2/a_6=-(a^{\prime}_2)^3/a^{\prime}_6\), then \((a^{\prime}_2)^3/a^3_2=a^{\prime}_6/a_6\), hence there exists \(\mu\in \bar{K}^*\) such that \(a_2^{\prime}=\mu^2a_2\) and \(a_6^{\prime}=\mu^6a_6\).

If \(j=0\), we use the curve \(E:y^2=x^3+x\). Otherwise, we consider the curve \[ y^2+xy=x^3-\frac{1}{j_0}. \]

Let elliptic curve \(E\) and \(E^{\prime}\) have the same \(j-\) invariant. Consider the following two cases:

Case I. \(j(E)\neq0\). In this case \(E\) and \(E^{\prime}\) have Weierstrass equations of the form \[ y^2=x^3+a_2x^2+a_6. \] The only substitutions preserving this type of equation are \[ x=u^2x^\prime \qquad \text{and} \qquad y=u^3y^{\prime}. \]

Case II. \(j(E)=0\). In this case \(E\) and \(E^{\prime}\) have Weierstrass equations of the form \[ y^2=x^3+a_4x+a_6. \] The substitutions preserving this form look like \[ x=u^2x^{\prime}+r \qquad \text{and} \qquad y=u^3y^{\prime}. \] An isomorphism from \(E\) to \(E^{\prime}\) is obtained by choosing \(u\) and \(r\) to satisfy \[ u^4=a_4^{\prime} \qquad \text{and} \qquad r^3+a_4r+a_6-u^6a_6^{\prime}=0. \]

2.19

Since \((x,y)\) and \(\alpha(x,y)\) lie on \(E\), then \[ \frac{(x^3+Ax+B)s(x)^2}{t(x)^2}=\frac{p(x)^3+Ap(x)q(x)^2+Bq(x)^3}{q(x)^3}. \] Let \(u(x)=p(x)^3+Ap(x)q(x)^2+Bq(x)^3\), if \(q\) and \(u\) have common root, then \(p\) and \(q\) have common root, which is not allowed.

From (a), we obtain \[ (x^3+Ax+B)s(x)^2q(x)^3=u(x)t(x)^2. \] Since \(t(x_0)=0\), so the multiplicity of \(x_0\) over \(u(x)t(x)^2\) at least 2. Notice that \(x^3+Ax+B\) has no multiple roots and \(s(x_0)\neq 0\), hence \(x_0\) is a root of \(q(x)\), that is \(q(x_0)=0\).

2.20

Let \(f(x,y)=x^3+ax^2-y^2=0\) describe a curve \(C\) in the affine plane and let \[ x=t,\qquad y=mt \] be a line \(L\) written in terms of the parameter \(t\). Let \(\tilde{f}(t)=f(t,mt)=t^3+(a-m^2)t^2\) and \(t=0\) correspond to point \((0,0)\), thus 2 is the highest power of \(t\) that divides \(\tilde{f}(t)\), hence \(L\) always intersects the curve to order at least 2. If the order is 3, then \(t^3\mid t^3+(a-m^2)t^2\), that is exactly when \(a=m^2\).

2.21

Let the line through the point \((-1,0)\) with slope \(m\): \(u=-1+m, v=tm\). Substitute the equations into circle equation, this yields \((t^2+1)m^2-2m=0\). Discarding the solution \(m=0\), then we obtain \(m=2/(t^2+1)\). Thus, \[ u=-1+\frac{2}{t^2+1}=\frac{t^2-1}{t^2+1},\qquad v=\frac{2t}{1+t^2}. \]

Let \(t=m/n\) in the expression of \(u\) and \(v\) by the parameter \(t\), then \[ u=\frac{t^2-1}{t^2+1}=\frac{m^2-n^2}{m^2+n^2},\qquad v=\frac{2mn}{m^2+n^2}. \] Let \(u=y/z\) and \(v=x/z\), thus there are integers \(m,n\) such that \[ x=2mn,\qquad y=m^2-n^2,\qquad z=m^2+n^2. \] If \(\gcd(m,n)=k\neq 1\), then \(k^2\mid \gcd(x,y,z)\neq1\), which is not allowed, thus \(\gcd(m,n)=1\). If \(m\equiv n\pmod 2\), then there exists integer \(q\) such that \(m=n+2q\), this leads to \(2\mid \gcd(x,y,z)\), which is a contradiction, hence \(m\not\equiv n \pmod 2\).

2.22

Notice that \[ \frac{d}{dx}\left(\frac{p(x)}{q(x)}\right)=\frac{p^{\prime}(x)q(x)-q^{\prime}(x)p(x)}{q^2(x)}. \] 1. If \(p^{\prime}(x)=q^{\prime}(x)=0\), it is obvious that \(\frac{d}{dx}\left(\frac{p(x)}{q(x)}\right)=0\).

  1. If \(\frac{d}{dx}\left(\frac{p(x)}{q(x)}\right)=0\), then we obtain \[ \frac{p^{\prime}(x)}{q^\prime(x)}=\frac{p(x)}{q(x)}=h(x). \] The equation is equivalent to \[ p^\prime(x)=h(x)q^\prime(x)\qquad\text{and}\qquad p(x)=h(x)q(x), \] this yields \(h(x)=e^x\). If \(p^\prime(x)\neq 0\), then \(q^\prime(x)\neq 0\). But \(e^xq(x)\) is not a polynomial, it is impossible. Thus \(p^\prime(x)=0\), then \(q^\prime(x)=0\).

2.23

\[ j(E^{(d)})=108\frac{(4Ad^2)^3}{4(Ad^2)^3+27(Bd^3)^2}=108\frac{(4A)^3}{4A^3+27B^2}=j(E). \]

The change of variables \(x=dx_1, y=\pm d\sqrt{d}y_1\) transform \(E^{(d)}: y^2=x^3+Ad^2x+Bd^3\) into \(E: y_1^2=x_1^3+Ax_1+B\).

The change of variables \(x=dx_1, y=\pm d^2y_1\) transform \(E^{(d)}: y^2=x^3+Ad^2x+Bd^3\) into the form \(dy_1^2=x_1^3+Ax_1+B\).

2.24

Since \(\gcd(\alpha,\beta)=1\), so there exist integers \(p,q\) such that \(p\alpha+q\beta=1\). If cubic polynomial \(x(x-\alpha)(x-\beta)\) have a triple root, then \(p\mid \alpha\) and \(p\mid \beta\). This yields \(p\mid p\alpha+q\beta=1\), which is not allowed.

Directly calculate.

Since \(\beta \equiv 0\pmod {32}\), then \(\alpha\beta/16 \equiv 0 \pmod 2\). Thus \(E_1\) has the form \(y_1^2+x_1y_1=x_1^3+ex_1^2\) for some \(e\in\mathbf{F}_2\). This curve is singular at \((0,0)\).

Let \(\tilde{f}(x_1,\gamma x_1)=x_1^3+(e-\gamma^2-\gamma)x_1^2\) and notice that the line \(y_1=\gamma x_1\) intersect \(E_1\) at \((0,0)\). It is easy to see that if \(\gamma^2+\gamma=e\), then the line intersects in part (c) to order 3, and if \(\gamma^2+\gamma \neq e\), then the line intersects in part (c) to order 2.

The equation \(\gamma^2+\gamma-e=0\) has two solutions \(x_1,x_2\) in \(\mathbf{\overline{F}}_2\), if \(x_1=x_2\), then \((\gamma-x_1)^2=\gamma^2+\gamma-e\). But \[ \frac{d}{d\gamma}(\gamma-x_1)^2=0, \qquad \frac{d}{d\gamma}(\gamma^2+\gamma-e)=1\neq 0, \] which is not allowed, hence \(x_1\neq x_2\).
